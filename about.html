About AFMASA

Our Story, Mission, and Vision for Africa's Maritime Future

Our History

Established as the West Africa Shipping Assembly in 2019, AFMASA began as a regional platform to foster cooperation among West African maritime stakeholders. Building on the success of this initiative, the assembly expanded its scope in 2025 to encompass the entire African continent, becoming the Africa Maritime and Shipping Assembly (AfMASA).

Today, AFMASA serves as the premier platform to foster intra-African trade through enhanced shipping and maritime cooperation. We bring together government officials, shipping lines, port operators, logistics providers, and maritime professionals to drive innovation, strengthen partnerships, and advance the maritime sector across Africa.

Mission

To foster intra-African trade through enhanced shipping and maritime cooperation, creating a platform that brings together industry leaders, government officials, and regulatory bodies to drive innovation and strengthen partnerships across the continent.

Vision

To establish AFMASA as the premier platform for maritime excellence and cooperation across Africa, facilitating seamless shipping connectivity, reducing trade barriers, and positioning African maritime operators as globally competitive leaders.

Core Values

Collaboration • Excellence • Innovation • Integrity • Inclusivity • Sustainability

Why AFMASA Matters to Africa's Maritime Future

⚓ Fostering Cooperation

We bring together diverse maritime stakeholders—shipping lines, ports, regulators, and logistics providers—to collaborate on shared challenges and drive collective solutions.

📊 Market Access

We provide market intelligence and connect African maritime businesses with opportunities across the continent and globally.

🌊 Capacity Building

We develop maritime expertise through training, best practices sharing, and professional development for shipping and logistics professionals.

🤝 Trade Facilitation

We work to reduce trade barriers, streamline shipping processes, and promote intra-African commerce through enhanced maritime connectivity.
