About AfMASA

Bridging the Governance Gap in Africa’s Maritime Sector

Who We Are

The African maritime sector faces complex challenges that require continuity, coordination, and private sector agility. AfMASA fills the gap left by sporadic policy focus and national regulatory “turf wars” by providing industry expertise and a persistent focus on core issues such as standards, financing, and anti-corruption.

As a continent-wide, private sector-driven platform, AfMASA advances practical solutions that strengthen maritime governance, enhance trade competitiveness, and unlock sustainable investment across Africa’s shipping ecosystem.

Our Mission

To advocate for regulatory harmonization, drive digital transparency, and facilitate investment that enhances the economic viability and global competitiveness of African shipping.

Governance & Structure

AfMASA is incorporated as a non-profit association with a presence in key African trade hubs including Nigeria, Kenya, South Africa, Egypt, and Morocco. Our structure ensures regional balance and continent-wide legitimacy.

The Secretariat

Based across key African trade hubs, the Secretariat serves as the administrative backbone of AfMASA, driving stakeholder engagement, membership coordination, and implementation of strategic initiatives.

Leadership & Representation

Advisory Board

Our Advisory Board reflects balanced representation from North, South, East, West, and Central Africa, ensuring that AfMASA’s decisions carry continent-wide credibility and buy-in.

Maritime Finance & Investment

Through our Technical Working Groups, we focus on de-risking public-private partnerships (PPPs) for ports, fleet acquisition, and maritime infrastructure development.

Regulatory Harmonization

We work toward standardizing documentation, aligning customs procedures, and supporting AfCFTA implementation to reduce trade friction across borders.

Digitalization & Human Capital

AfMASA promotes anti-corruption initiatives, digital port systems, standardized maritime training curricula, and enhanced seafarer welfare to build a transparent and competitive maritime ecosystem.
