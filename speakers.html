Featured Speakers

Leading maritime professionals from across Africa and the globe

AFMASA 2025 Speakers

September 18, 2025 • London, UK • International Maritime Organisation

Dr. Adegboyega Oyetola (CON)

Honorable Minister of Marine and Blue Economy, Nigeria

Dr. Adegboyega Oyetola brings extensive experience in maritime policy and blue economy development. As Nigeria's Minister, he champions intra-African maritime cooperation and regional shipping excellence.

Aminu Umar

President, Nigerian Chamber of Shipping & Board Member, International Chamber of Shipping

Aminu Umar represents the interests of Nigerian shipping operators and brings global perspective through his role at the International Chamber of Shipping, advocating for maritime industry standards and cooperation.

Dr. Emeka Akabogu

Senior Partner, Akabogu & Associates

Dr. Emeka Akabogu is a leading maritime law expert and founder of Akabogu & Associates, the organization behind AFMASA. His expertise in shipping law shapes Africa's maritime regulatory landscape.

Iroghama Ogbeifun

CEO, Starz Investments Company, Nigeria

Iroghama Ogbeifun leads Starz Investments, focusing on strategic maritime investments and business opportunities across the African shipping sector.

Mostafa Benhima

General Manager, Grimaldi Agencies Maroc, Morocco

Mostafa Benhima brings expertise in North African maritime operations and represents the Grimaldi Group's significant presence in African shipping and port operations.

Capt. Nicolas Bernard

Managing Director, NBC Maritime, Nigeria

Captain Nicolas Bernard brings operational maritime expertise through NBC Maritime, specializing in shipping operations and maritime services across West Africa.

Nada Eldib

Business Director, Eldib Advocates, Egypt

Nada Eldib represents Egypt's growing role in maritime law and shipping services, bringing North African and Suez Canal corridor expertise to African maritime discussions.

Cecilia Müller

CEO, Maritime Anti-Corruption Network (Torbrand)

Cecilia Müller brings global perspective on maritime compliance and anti-corruption initiatives, ensuring ethical standards in African shipping and maritime operations.
